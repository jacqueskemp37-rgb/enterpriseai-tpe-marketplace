# Operational Driver Skill — "How the Business Actually Runs"

## Purpose
The Operational Driver ensures the company can DELIVER on its strategy.
Covers processes, technology, customer service, and the operational
backbone that makes everything else possible.

Serves Strategic Direction dimensions:
- Distribution Channels (operations must support channel delivery)
- Reach/Geography (operations must scale to new markets)
- Business Model (operations determine cost structure and scalability)


## Required Sections

### 5.1 Process Optimization
- Map core processes (order-to-cash, procure-to-pay, etc.)
- Identify bottlenecks with quantified impact
- Lean management and Six Sigma opportunities
- Automation candidates with ROI estimates
- Process KPIs: cycle time, error rate, cost per transaction

| Process | Current Cycle Time | Target | Automation Potential | Investment |
|---------|-------------------|--------|---------------------|------------|

### 5.2 IT and Infrastructure
- Current technology landscape (systems, platforms, integrations)
- Technical debt assessment and remediation plan
- Digital transformation roadmap per year (2025-2028)
- AI adoption strategy: where AI adds most value
- Cybersecurity posture: current state, required investments
- Cloud strategy: on-premise vs. cloud vs. hybrid

| System | Purpose | Age | Replace By | Investment | Priority |
|--------|---------|-----|-----------|------------|----------|

### 5.3 Customer Service Model
- Current model: channels, SLAs, satisfaction scores
- Target model: omnichannel, AI-driven, self-service
- Customer journey mapping with pain points identified
- Service level targets per year (2025-2028)
- Tech enablers: chatbots, CRM, knowledge base, ticketing

### 5.4 Supply Chain and Procurement (if applicable)
- Supplier landscape and concentration risk
- Procurement optimization opportunities
- Inventory management strategy
- Logistics and distribution efficiency
- Sustainability in supply chain


## Cross-Driver Alignment (MANDATORY)

Operational MUST align with:
- FINANCIAL: IT investment and opex must match approved budgets
- ORGANIZATIONAL: Operations headcount must match HR plan
- MARKETING: Service promises must be operationally deliverable
- PORTFOLIO: New business lines need operational capacity planning
- REPUTATION: Service quality directly impacts brand reputation

Operational must trace back to these Strategic Direction dimensions:
- Distribution Channels: operations deliver through these channels
- Reach/Geography: operations must work in each target market
- Business Model: operations determine unit economics


## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Core processes identified and mapped (at least top 5)
- [ ] IT landscape documented with replacement timeline
- [ ] Cybersecurity explicitly addressed with budget
- [ ] Customer service has measurable SLAs and targets
- [ ] AI/automation opportunities quantified with ROI
- [ ] All investments cross-reference Financial Driver capex
- [ ] Strategic Direction dimensions are traced


## Edge Cases
- SaaS/digital-native: No physical supply chain. Focus on platform
  reliability (uptime SLAs), deployment frequency, DevOps maturity.
- Manufacturing: Heavy supply chain, lean, quality. Include OEE metrics.
- Professional services: Operations = project delivery methodology,
  utilization rates, knowledge management, QA.
