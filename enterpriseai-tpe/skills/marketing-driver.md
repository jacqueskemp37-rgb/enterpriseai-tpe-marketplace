# Marketing Driver Skill — "Who Are Your Customers and How Do You Reach Them"

## Purpose
The Marketing Driver defines WHO the company serves, WHAT it offers them,
and through WHICH channels. It bridges the Portfolio strategy with actual
market execution.

Serves Strategic Direction dimensions:
- Customer Segments (primary)
- Products/Services (shared with Portfolio)
- Distribution Channels (primary)
- Reach/Geography (shared with Portfolio and Operations)


## Required Sections

### 3.1 Customer Target Groups
- Define segments with specificity (not "SMEs" but "manufacturing SMEs,
  50-250 employees, Benelux region")
- Per segment: size, growth rate, current penetration, target penetration
- Prioritize: primary, secondary, aspirational segments
- Customer personas for top 3 segments (role, needs, pain points, decision)
- Digital vs. traditional behavior per segment

| Segment | Size | Growth | Current Share | Target 2028 | Priority |
|---------|------|--------|--------------|-------------|----------|

### 3.2 Products and Services
- Current portfolio mapped to customer segments
- Gap analysis: which segments are underserved?
- New product/service roadmap per year (2025-2028)
- Pricing strategy per product/segment combination
- Competitive differentiation per product

| Product/Service | Target Segment | Revenue 2025 | Pricing Model | Differentiator |
|-----------------|---------------|-------------|---------------|----------------|

### 3.3 Distribution Channels
- Current channels: B2C, B2B, online, retail, third-party, partners, API
- Performance per channel: revenue, cost-to-serve, customer satisfaction
- Channel strategy 2025-2028: expand, optimize, or retire
- Digital transformation of channels (e-commerce, self-service)
- Channel conflict management

| Channel | Revenue | Cost-to-Serve | NPS | Strategy |
|---------|---------|--------------|-----|----------|

### 3.4 Geographic Reach (Market Expansion)
- Current geographic footprint with revenue per market
- Target markets for expansion with entry timeline
- Regulatory and cultural barriers per target market
- Localization requirements (language, compliance, operations)
- Market entry model: direct, partner, franchise, digital-first

| Market | Status | Revenue 2025 | Entry Year | Entry Model | Investment |
|--------|--------|-------------|-----------|-------------|------------|

### 3.5 Go-to-Market Execution
- Marketing budget allocation per channel and segment
- Campaign calendar with key milestones
- Sales process: lead generation, qualification, conversion, retention
- Marketing technology stack (CRM, automation, analytics)
- Content strategy and thought leadership plan


## Cross-Driver Alignment (MANDATORY)

Marketing MUST align with:
- PORTFOLIO: Products/services must match business line strategy
- FINANCIAL: Marketing spend must fit within approved budgets
- OPERATIONAL: Customer promises must be deliverable by operations
- REPUTATION: Marketing messaging must align with brand positioning
- ORGANIZATIONAL: Sales team capacity must match lead generation targets

Marketing must trace back to these Strategic Direction dimensions:
- Customer Segments: directly defines who to serve
- Products/Services: defines what to offer each segment
- Distribution Channels: defines how to reach each segment
- Reach/Geography: defines where to compete


## Quality Checklist
- [ ] At least 3 customer segments defined with measurable criteria
- [ ] Every product/service mapped to at least one segment
- [ ] Channel strategy covers both current and future state
- [ ] Geographic expansion has specific timeline and entry model
- [ ] Pricing rationale included (not just numbers)
- [ ] Go-to-market has specific timeline, not just aspirational goals
- [ ] Cross-references to Portfolio and Financial drivers are explicit
- [ ] All four Strategic Direction dimensions are addressed


## Edge Cases
- B2B: Longer sales cycle mapping, account-based marketing, reference selling.
- B2C: Volume metrics, conversion funnels, digital-first approach.
- Marketplace/Platform: Define both sides (buyers AND sellers) as separate
  segments with separate value propositions.
- Highly regulated (pharma, finance): Compliance gates in marketing approval.
