# Reputational Driver Skill — "How the World Sees You"

## Purpose
The Reputational Driver manages the company's brand, communications, legal
standing, and stakeholder relationships. It protects and enhances the firm's
license to operate and market perception.

Serves Strategic Direction dimensions:
- Customer Segments (brand must resonate with target segments)
- Reach/Geography (reputation must be managed per market)


## Required Sections

### 6.1 Brand Strategy
- Brand positioning statement (for whom, what you offer, why different)
- Brand architecture (if multiple brands/sub-brands)
- Online brand strategy: website, SEO, digital presence
- Offline brand strategy: events, publications, industry presence
- Brand metrics: awareness, consideration, preference measurement

### 6.2 External Communication
- Media strategy: press relations, thought leadership, analyst relations
- Social media strategy per platform with objectives and KPIs
- Sponsoring and free publicity opportunities
- Crisis communication plan (prepared responses, escalation matrix)
- Content calendar with themes per quarter

| Channel | Objective | Audience | Frequency | KPI | Budget |
|---------|-----------|----------|-----------|-----|--------|

### 6.3 Internal Communication
- Intranet strategy and employee engagement channels
- Town halls, leadership talks, team communication cadence
- Change management communication approach
- Employee advocacy program (employees as brand ambassadors)
- Feedback mechanisms: surveys, pulse checks, suggestion systems

### 6.4 Legal Affairs
- Regulatory landscape and compliance requirements
- Key legal risks and mitigation strategies
- Contract management approach
- Intellectual property protection strategy
- Data privacy and GDPR compliance status

### 6.5 Compliance and Sustainability
- Compliance framework: policies, training, monitoring
- ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) strategy
- Sustainability reporting and targets
- Industry-specific compliance requirements
- Audit schedule and remediation tracking


## Cross-Driver Alignment (MANDATORY)

Reputation MUST align with:
- MARKETING: Brand positioning must match marketing messaging exactly
- ORGANIZATIONAL: Culture and D&I must be authentic and proven
- FINANCIAL: Legal/compliance costs must be budgeted adequately
- OPERATIONAL: Service quality claims must match operational reality
- PORTFOLIO: Brand must stretch to cover new business lines


## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Brand positioning is specific and differentiated
- [ ] Crisis communication plan exists with named spokespersons
- [ ] Legal section covers top 3 regulatory risks for the industry
- [ ] Internal comms includes feedback loops (not just top-down)
- [ ] ESG/sustainability has measurable targets
- [ ] All costs cross-reference Financial Driver


## Edge Cases
- B2B: Less consumer-facing brand, more thought leadership, analyst
  relations, industry reputation, reference clients.
- Highly regulated (banking, pharma): Compliance section dominates.
  Include regulatory relationship management.
- Startup: Brand built from scratch. Focus on founding story,
  differentiation, awareness-building with limited budget.
