# /tpe:onboard — TPE Introduction and Training

## What This Command Does
Creates a training presentation that introduces TPE methodology to new
team members, executives, or stakeholders who need to understand the
framework before participating in business planning.

## Input Required
- Target audience: executives, managers, new hires, board members?
- Company name (to customize examples)
- Depth level: overview (10 min) or detailed (30 min)?

## Execution Workflow

### Step 1: Assess Audience Level
- Executives: focus on strategic value, ROI, decision-making power
- Managers: focus on their driver responsibilities, cross-alignment
- New hires: focus on how TPE structures their work
- Board: focus on governance, oversight, performance monitoring

### Step 2: Produce Training Deck

SLIDE STRUCTURE (Overview — 10-12 slides):

Slide 1: TITLE
"Introduction to the TPE Business Planning Framework"
EnterpriseAI — Complete, Consistent, Comprehensive
[Company name] | [Date]

Slide 2: THE PROBLEM
- Most companies plan in silos
- Departments make independent plans that contradict each other
- Example: Marketing targets 50K customers, Operations can handle 30K
- Result: wasted resources, missed targets, organizational friction

Slide 3: THE TPE SOLUTION
- One framework that covers the ENTIRE organization
- 3C: Complete (every dimension), Consistent (all aligned),
  Comprehensive (deep enough to act on)
- Published methodology: Routledge, HBR/Ivey validated

Slide 4: THE THREE LAYERS
- Layer 1: Strategic Context (WHAT — where are we going?)
- Layer 2: Execution Plans (HOW — six driver plans)
- Layer 3: Performance (RESULT — KPIs and tracking)
- Visual: pyramid or three-layer diagram

Slide 5: SIX DIMENSIONS OF STRATEGIC DIRECTION
- Line of Business, Products/Services, Distribution Channels,
  Customer Segments, Reach/Geography, Business Model
- These define the playing field BEFORE planning begins
- Visual: hexagon or wheel with 6 dimensions

Slide 6: THE SIX DRIVERS
- Portfolio, Marketing, Organizational, Operational, Reputation, Financial
- Each driver has an owner, a plan, and cross-references to other drivers
- Financial is the integration driver — if numbers don't work, plan doesn't work
- Visual: six interconnected circles

Slide 7: ALIGNMENT — THE KEY DIFFERENTIATOR
- Every driver must reference at least 2 other drivers
- Six specific alignment checks run automatically
- Conflicts are flagged and resolved before execution begins
- Example: show a simple alignment matrix

Slide 8: THE LIVING DOCUMENT
- TPE plans are not filed away — they are continuously updated
- Weekly, monthly, quarterly review cadence
- KPI dashboard with real-time RAG status
- AI-powered: Claude monitors alignment and flags issues

Slide 9: YOUR ROLE IN TPE
[Customize per audience:]
- As a driver owner: maintain your plan, update KPIs, flag cross-driver issues
- As an executive: review alignment, resolve conflicts, approve changes
- As a team member: understand how your work connects to the bigger picture

Slide 10: HOW TO USE THE PLUGIN
- /tpe:quick-scan — start here to assess current state
- /tpe:full-plan — build or rebuild the complete plan
- /tpe:driver-audit — check quality of existing plans
- /tpe:alignment-check — verify cross-driver consistency
- /tpe:kpi-dashboard — track performance
- /tpe:scheduled-review — set up recurring reviews

Slide 11: GETTING STARTED
- Step 1: Run /tpe:quick-scan (10 minutes)
- Step 2: Review results with leadership team
- Step 3: Decide scope (full plan or targeted improvement)
- Step 4: Execute with Claude guiding the process

Slide 12: QUESTIONS AND NEXT STEPS
Contact information
Link to full TPE documentation
Scheduled follow-up date

## Customization Rules
- Always use company name in examples where possible
- If company has completed a quick-scan: include their RAG results
- If specific drivers are known to be weak: emphasize those in examples
- Adapt language to audience level (no jargon for board, technical for managers)
