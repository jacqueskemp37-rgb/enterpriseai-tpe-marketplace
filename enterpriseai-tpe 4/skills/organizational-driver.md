# Organizational Driver Skill — "Who Does What, and How Are They Organized"

## Purpose
The Organizational Driver defines the human architecture of the firm:
structure, people, culture, and governance. It ensures the organization
has the right people in the right roles to execute the strategy.

Serves Strategic Direction dimensions: ALL (people enable every dimension)


## Required Sections

### 4.1 Organization Chart — Matrix Format
- Clear org chart showing reporting lines
- Matrix format for complex orgs (functional x business unit)
- Key leadership positions and current occupants
- Span of control analysis (direct reports per manager)
- Single points of failure (critical roles with no backup)

### 4.2 Human Resources Policy
- Headcount plan per year (2025-2028) by department/function
- Recruitment strategy: sources, timeline, cost-per-hire targets
- Training and development: budget per employee, required programs
- Onboarding process for new hires
- Retention strategy: what keeps top talent

| Department | HC 2025 | HC 2026 | HC 2027 | HC 2028 | Key Hires |
|------------|---------|---------|---------|---------|-----------|

### 4.3 Compensation Policy
- Salary bands per level/function
- Bonus structure: criteria, targets, payout mechanics
- Benefits package overview
- Market benchmarking (which percentile targeted?)
- Long-term incentives (equity, profit sharing) if applicable

### 4.4 Management Development Tools
- Succession planning: identified successors for top 10 positions
- Management development program structure
- Culture definition: stated values AND observed behaviors
- Performance review process and cadence
- Leadership competency framework

### 4.5 Diversity, Equity and Inclusion
- Current D&I metrics (gender, background, age distribution)
- D&I targets per year (2025-2028)
- Specific programs and initiatives
- Accountability: who owns D&I outcomes?
- Inclusion measurement (belonging surveys, participation rates)


## Cross-Driver Alignment (MANDATORY)

Organizational MUST align with:
- PORTFOLIO: Growth/M&A plans require hiring and integration plans
- OPERATIONAL: Headcount must support operational capacity requirements
- FINANCIAL: Total people cost must match compensation budgets
- MARKETING: Sales team size must support go-to-market targets
- REPUTATION: Culture and D&I must support brand values authentically


## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Org chart is current and shows actual reporting lines
- [ ] Headcount plan has specific numbers per year, not ranges
- [ ] Compensation benchmarked against named competitors
- [ ] Succession plan covers at least CEO and direct reports
- [ ] D&I has measurable targets, not just aspirational statements
- [ ] Total people cost ties back to Financial Driver


## Edge Cases
- Startup (under 20): Flat org. Focus on which roles to hire first.
  Culture section = founder values codification.
- Post-M&A: Integration org chart, retention packages for key acquired
  talent, culture harmonization plan.
- Remote/hybrid: Remote work policy, collaboration tools, distributed
  culture maintenance.
