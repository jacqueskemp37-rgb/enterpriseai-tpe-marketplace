# Portfolio Driver Skill — "How to Grow Your Business"

## Purpose
The Portfolio Driver answers the fundamental strategic question: WHERE will
growth come from? It evaluates current business lines, identifies expansion
opportunities, and determines which units to invest in, fix, or exit.

Serves Strategic Direction dimensions:
- Line of Business (primary)
- Products/Services (shared with Marketing)
- Reach/Geography (shared with Marketing and Operations)


## Required Sections

### 2.1 Existing and New Business Lines
- Evaluate current business lines with revenue, margin, growth trajectory
- Classify each line: core, adjacent, or transformational
- Market positioning per line (leader/challenger/niche)
- For each line: GROW, MAINTAIN, FIX, or EXIT recommendation

| Business Line | Revenue 2025 | Margin | Growth | Position | Recommendation |
|---------------|-------------|--------|--------|----------|----------------|

### 2.2 Organic Growth
- Assess performance of existing business units
- Identify organic growth levers per unit
- Set growth targets per year (2025-2028)
- Required investments to achieve organic growth

### 2.3 Mergers and Acquisitions (M&A)
- Identify potential acquisition targets and rationale
- Valuation criteria and budget envelope
- Integration approach (full, partial, standalone)
- Timeline and milestones
- Risk assessment per potential target

### 2.4 Strategic Partnerships
- Current partnerships: assess value and strategic fit
- Gap analysis: which capabilities are missing?
- Target partner profiles and selection criteria
- Partnership models: JV, licensing, distribution, technology
- Name specific target partners where possible

### 2.5 Fix or Exit Underperforming Units
- Identify units below target performance
- Root cause analysis per unit
- Fix plan with timeline and investment required
- Exit criteria: when to divest vs. persist
- Impact on rest of portfolio if unit is exited


## Cross-Driver Alignment (MANDATORY)

Portfolio MUST align with:
- FINANCIAL: Growth targets must match revenue projections and capex budgets
- MARKETING: New business lines need defined customer segments and channels
- ORGANIZATIONAL: Growth/M&A requires headcount and capability planning
- OPERATIONAL: New lines require operational capacity and IT support

Portfolio must trace back to these Strategic Direction dimensions:
- Line of Business: directly defines what lines to grow/exit
- Products/Services: portfolio determines what is offered
- Reach/Geography: portfolio expansion drives geographic reach


## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Every business line has a GROW/MAINTAIN/FIX/EXIT recommendation
- [ ] Organic growth targets set for each year 2025-2028
- [ ] M&A section addresses at least feasibility even if not applicable
- [ ] Partnerships are named specifically, not just described generically
- [ ] Fix/Exit decisions include financial impact analysis
- [ ] All targets cross-reference Financial Driver projections
- [ ] Links back to Strategic Direction dimensions are explicit


## Edge Cases
- Startup with one business line: Focus on growth vectors within that line
  plus adjacent opportunities. M&A section becomes "potential future."
- Conglomerate with 20+ units: Group into clusters, analyze at cluster level,
  deep-dive top 5 by revenue and bottom 3 by performance.
- Non-profit: Replace "revenue" with "impact metrics" and "margin" with
  "cost efficiency." Growth means expanded reach, not profit.
